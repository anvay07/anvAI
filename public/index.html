How it feels

Three things you'll notice before you notice anvAI noticing you.

It notices before you say it.

When your words get careful — shorter sentences, a joke where a feeling should be — anvAI slows down too. It's reading intensity and defense as much as content, so it doesn't force positivity onto a sentence that's still guarding itself.

It knows when to stop talking.

No stacked questions, no bulleted advice before your feeling's been heard. One thought at a time, at your pace — sometimes the most useful response is a short one that leaves room for you to keep going.

It knows when you need more than it can give.

If things get heavier than a conversation can hold, anvAI says so plainly and puts a real person within reach — no walls of text between you and help when you need it fastest.

Worth saying plainly

anvAI is a bridge, not a replacement. If you're already working with a therapist, keep going — this is for the hours in between. If you're not, and you're scared to start, this is a lower step onto that ladder, not the top of it.

If you're in crisis, anvAI will say so and hand you to a real person immediately. So will the pill in the corner of this page, any time.
